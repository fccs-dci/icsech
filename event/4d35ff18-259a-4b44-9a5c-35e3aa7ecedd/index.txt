15:T113c,<p><span style="font-size:12pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif"><span style="background:white"><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:#222222">The history of pre-Qin Classical Chinese thought is usually woven around a small set of great philosophers, each taken as the authorial voice behind a single text: </span></span></span><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black">Kongzi (the <i>Lunyu</i>), Laozi (<i>Daodejing</i>), Mozi, Mengzi, Zhuangzi (the “Inner Chapters” of the <i>Zhuangzi</i>), Xunzi, and Hanfeizi. Does this view of texts and authors fit the reality of how texts were written and formed in early China? Based on archeological evidence from recently excavated texts and greater attention to the role of Han dynasty scholars in editing and forming texts, many scholars of early China have argued that it does not. In this faculty dialogue, Tao Jiang, Esther Sunkyung Klein, and Franklin Perkins will explore the implications of that conclusion for how we should read and interpret classical Chinese philosophical texts. </span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:12pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif"><b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black">Tao Jiang</span></span></b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black"> is Professor of Chinese and Buddhist Philosophies at Rutgers University, New Brunswick, NJ. Jiang is chair of Religion Department and director of Center for Chinese Studies at Rutgers.</span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size:12pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif"><b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black">Esther Sunkyung Klein</span></span></b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black"> (PhD Princeton, East Asian Studies 2010) is Senior Lecturer at The Australian National University. <p><span style="font-size:12pt"><span style="font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif"><b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black">Franklin Perkins</span></span></b><span style="font-family:&quot;Garamond&quot;,serif"><span style="color:black"> is Professor of Philosophy at UHM and editor of the journal <i>Philosophy East and West</i>.
