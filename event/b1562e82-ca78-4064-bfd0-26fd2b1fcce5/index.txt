17:T408,<p dir="ltr">On Thursday, November 13 from 5:00-6:30pm EST, please join us at The New School for an in-person&nbsp; panel discussion on the shifting landscapes of news media in India, China, the United States and the United Kingdom. An authoritarian turn in countries around the world is eroding older norms of press freedom.&nbsp; A varied playbook of intimidating journalists includes the use of laws on libel to sanction commercial print media outlets, withdrawal of funding, surveillance, trolling and physical attacks on journalists, and the criminalization of dissent under vague national security and defamation laws. Panelists will discuss how regimes of India, China, the United States and the UK have tried to restrict if not control what journalists and writers produce, as well as coping strategies that are being deployed in response.</p>
