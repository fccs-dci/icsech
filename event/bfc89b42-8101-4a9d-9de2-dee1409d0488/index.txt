In Korea, the Constitutional Court is hearing arguments over whether to ratify the legislature’s impeachment of President Yoon Suk Yeol. But until impeaching Yoon, the opposition party that controls the legislature was refusing to fill vacancies on the court. In Taiwan, the party that controls the legislature has blocked the president’s nominees to the Constitutional Court because it regards the court as politicized, while also passing legislation that immobilizes the court unless vacancies are filled.
