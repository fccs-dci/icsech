Wang explores the Lower Yangzi Delta, a “world of water,” and unravels how rural communities sustained their water resources through self-organized hydraulic institutions and under minimal state intervention. Gao shifts the focus to Qing Xinjiang’s arid frontier, investigating how karez irrigation shaped property rights, agrarian practices, and imperial governance in Turpan.
